MigraTracker

Brief

Created for WNM 617: Mobile Web Technology 1 in Fall 2017 under Instructor Matthew David. The goal of MigraTracker was to create a tracker app that followed the movements and activities of the specific animals. The focus was on making the app user-friendly for adults with a secondary emphasis on young adults who have an interest in tracking animals. The animal focused on was the Mandrill. Four mandrills from the San Diego Zoo to be specific, Jasper, Kesi, Ajani, and JJ. The photos were obtained on a research trip to the zoo in 2017. The images of JJ were obtained during an undergraduate research trip in 2015 as he has since been removed from the enclosure to allow the parents freedom with their baby.

Typeface

Aa

For MigraTracker the goal was to keep it to one typeface for simplicity and legibility. The typeface chosen was Open Sans which was designed by Steve Matteson for Google. Open Sans is a sans serif with large x-heights, open forms and an upright stress. This gives the type a neutral and friendly appearance as well as making it legible for the web and mobile devices.

Technology

The wireframes were created with pen and paper before being uploaded to a computer for the creation of low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes in Adobe Illustrator. The prototypes were then finished in Adobe XD to create a cohesive and comprehensive prototype for each screen. App

The MigraTracker app was created with users in mind. The design was meant for anyone to easily navigate through the pages. The overall app is geared more towards a casual tracker who has an interest in Mandrills as well as keeping up-to-date on their movements and activities.

The design was created as a mobile app or mobile website using a multi-page jQuery format. The user can add the app icon to their home screen via the sharing feature on mobile devices or as a bookmark on desktop/laptops.
